# Theme 3 — Carbon + Amber

**Mood**: Warm, premium, considered. The Hermès / Aesop / boutique-firm register — a warm near-black background instead of cool slate, a single amber "seal" mark that reads like a wax stamp, and editorial typography. Reads as bespoke craft rather than commodity SaaS.

**Who it's for**: Boutique advisory practices, design-led agencies, family offices, premium dev shops that bill on retainer. The buyer who would rather pay 30% more for software that doesn't look like every other dashboard. Also: anyone whose marketing position is "we are not the cheap option, we are the considered option".

**The trade-off**: This is the highest-personality choice and therefore the highest-risk. Warm carbon backgrounds are unusual in B2B SaaS — they will feel either distinctively premium or off-trend depending on the buyer. Amber is also load-bearing as both brand and warning semantic in many systems; we resolve that here by shifting the confidence palette to lime/orange/red, but it adds one extra rule to remember when designing new components. Tightly-kerned Inter SemiBold at headline sizes needs more typographic discipline than Theme 1's looser Inter Bold.

**Why not lead**: Highest variance in how buyers will react. If three design-partner CFOs see this and one calls it "warm and elegant" while two call it "weird", we have a problem. Save for v1.1 unless the founder has strong conviction that the boutique register is right for the buyer.
