## 1. Target Segments

We are looking for **5–30-person professional services firms** that are actively feeling billing and AR pain. Decision-maker is the founder, COO, or finance lead — not the bookkeeper.

Current-stack signal to look for: QBO or Xero + Toggl or Harvest; or any public complaint about BQE Core, Mavenlink, Harvest billing complexity, or "spreadsheets and QuickBooks."

### Priority Order

**Tier 1 — Highest conversion likelihood**

- **US boutique management consulting / advisory** (5–20 people). Partners-model firms where billing is retainer + milestone + T&M mixed. Common pain: engagement letters in Word, invoices copy-pasted from a template, AR chased manually. LinkedIn-dense, SaaS-buying culture, comfortable with early tools.

- **US IT consulting / dev shops / fractional CTO firms** (5–30 people). Billing is hourly or fixed-scope. Pain: time tracking fragmented from invoicing; scope creep is invisible until project is over. Very active on Twitter/X and Indie Hackers. Will test a product early if the demo is compelling.

**Tier 2 — High value, slightly more conservative**

- **UK professional services** (any vertical above, 5–30 people). Similar pains; slightly more cautious about early-stage products. Target firms complaining about Xero's workflow gaps or FreeAgent limitations. LinkedIn is the primary channel; email cold outreach converts well.

- **Corporate finance / M&A advisory boutiques** (US/UK, 5–15 people). Engagement letters are long and compliance-sensitive — the AI extraction angle is very compelling here. Decision cycle is longer but LOI quality is high. Warm introductions work best.

**Tier 3 — Valid but lower Week-1 priority**

- **Accounting / CPA / bookkeeping firms with project work** (US, 5–20 people). Self-referential angle is strong — they understand double-entry, they'll stress-test the GL. Ideal early stress-testers but may be slower to trial something new.

- **Marketing / brand agencies** (US, 5–30 people). Billing is project-based + retainer. Pain is real but they're less likely to trust their books to an AI product without significant social proof. Target after Tier 1–2 cohorts are in.

### Out of Scope Week 1

- Law firms (regulatory constraints; longer decision cycle).
- India market (warm intros only; no cold outreach in Week 1).
- Firms > 50 people (too complex for v1 workflows; wrong buyer motion).

## 2. DM Template — Cold Outreach (Founder Personal Voice)

> **Channel**: LinkedIn DM or Twitter/X DM
> **Length**: ~120 words
> **Voice**: First-person founder. Honest, curious, specific. Not a product announcement. Not a sales pitch.

---

Hey [Name] — I saw [specific signal: recent post about invoicing chaos / your firm just hired a second PM / your tweet about Harvest pricing].

I'm building Aethos — an AI-native ERP for PS firms. The core idea: you drop your engagement letter or vendor invoice into a chat, and the AI does the data entry — extracts the terms, drafts the engagement, codes the expense to the right GL account. You just approve.

Stripe payouts, multi-currency, double-entry GL under the hood. The stuff that should just work, finally working.

I'm looking for 3 design-partner firms to build this with, not just for. Six months free, and I personally support the onboarding.

[Firm] feels like exactly the kind of practice we're building for. Open to 20 minutes this week?

— [Founder name]

---

**Personalisation notes for each send:**
- Replace `[specific signal]` with something real — a post, a hire, a tweet, a conference they spoke at. Do not send without this.
- Replace `[Firm]` with the actual firm name.
- Do not reference "changing the industry," "revolutionary," or "game-changing." Specific and honest wins.
- LinkedIn: send connection request first if not connected; DM within 24h of acceptance.
- Twitter: DM directly if they have DMs open; otherwise reply to a relevant tweet first, then DM.

## 4. Intro Call Agenda — 30-Minute Discovery Call

> **Format**: Video call (Zoom or Google Meet, founder-hosted)
> **Goal**: Understand their pain, show early product, ask for LOI

### Agenda

**Minutes 0–5: Intros + Their Firm Context**
- Founder: brief (30 seconds) on who they are and what they're building.
- Ask: "Tell me a bit about [Firm] — how many people, what kind of work, who owns the finance function?"
- Listen for: size, billing model (T&M / fixed / retainer), current tools, who does the books.

**Minutes 5–15: Walk the Pain**
The most important part of the call. Don't demo yet. Ask about their actual process.
- "Walk me through what happens after you finish a piece of client work — how does it turn into an invoice?"
- "How do you handle engagement letters? Is that in a template, or do you write them fresh?"
- "What's your AR situation like — do you have invoices sitting out more than 30 days?"
- "When a vendor or sub-contractor sends you an invoice, how does it get into your books?"
- Listen actively. Take notes. Repeat back: "So it sounds like the main friction is [X] — does that feel right?"
- Do not rush to "we solve that" — let them finish describing the pain before moving.

**Minutes 15–25: Early Product Demo / Wireframes**
- If v1 product is available: demo the copilot screen with a document drop → AI extraction → HITL approve flow.
- If not yet (Week 1–2): share wireframes or a Loom walkthrough of the intended experience.
- Lead with the pain they just described: "You mentioned engagement letters are the biggest friction — here's exactly how Aethos handles that."
- Show: document upload → agent extracts → HITL card → approve → engagement created. The whole loop in 2 minutes.
- Show confidence scores and the approve/edit/reject options — transparency is a feature.
- Do not over-demo. Show the one or two flows most relevant to their pain; save the rest for follow-up.

**Minutes 25–30: LOI Ask**
- "We have 3 design-partner slots. The deal is: 6 months free, white-glove migration, weekly 30-min call with me for 6 weeks. In return, you use it in real workflows and give us blunt feedback. Your input materially shapes the roadmap."
- "This isn't a beta sign-up form — I'm asking you personally. Is this something [Firm] would want to do?"
- If yes: "Great — I'll send you a short LOI, just a shared doc, not a legal contract. Sign it and we'll get you onboarded."
- If hesitant: "What would need to be true for it to be a yes?" — then listen. Do not pressure; note the concern and follow up.
- If no: "Totally fine. Can I ask what held you back? That's genuinely useful."

## 5. LOI Template — Letter of Intent

> **Format**: Shared Google Doc (not a legal contract). Plain language. No signatures required — a comment saying "I'm in" is enough.
> **Tone**: Warm, honest, mutual.

---

**Aethos for Professional Services — Design Partner Letter of Intent**

Date: [Date]
Firm: [Firm Name]
Contact: [Name, Title]
Aethos contact: [Founder Name]

---

**What this is**

This is an informal agreement between [Firm Name] and Aethos. It's not a legal contract — it's a mutual commitment to work together to build something useful. Either party can walk away with a conversation.

**What Aethos commits to**

- **3 months free Pro access** to Aethos for Professional Services, starting from your onboarding date. No trial card, no auto-billing.
- **White-glove onboarding**: we migrate your last 12 months of client, engagement, and invoice data into Aethos for you.
- **Direct founder line**: weekly 30-minute call with [Founder Name] for the first 6 weeks. Between calls, direct Slack or email access.
- **Roadmap influence**: your feedback is logged, discussed weekly, and materially shapes what gets built next. You'll see the plan; you can push back on it.
- After the 3 months: a **lifetime 50% discount** on the equivalent plan, as a thank-you for the early bet.

**What [Firm Name] commits to**

- **1 hour per week** of feedback (the 30-min weekly call + async responses to specific questions or prototypes we send over).
- **Real-workflow usage**: use Aethos for actual billing, expense tracking, or engagement management — not just a demo environment. Even if it's initially just one workflow or one client.
- **Honest feedback**: blunt is better than polite. If something doesn't work for your firm, say so early.
- **Case study permission** (optional): if Aethos works well for you, we'd love to reference [Firm Name] in our marketing — a logo, a quote, a short case study. This is opt-in; there's no obligation.

**Timeline**

- Onboarding begins: [Target date, typically within 1 week of LOI]
- Free access period: 3 months from onboarding date
- Weekly calls: 6 weeks (can extend by mutual agreement)

---

*Questions? Reply in this doc or email [founder@aethos.app].*

*[Founder Name] — Aethos*
